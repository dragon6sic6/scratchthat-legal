Scratch that

Privacy Policy

We can’t read your messages and we can’t see your photos. Not “won’t” — can’t. There is no account, no login, no analytics, no tracking, and no ads. This page explains plainly what happens to what you write and what you send.

No data reaches us

Scratch that has no accounts and no analytics. We don’t know who you are, who you message, or how often you use the app. Local counters (like which lines you’ve recently seen) exist only on your device and never leave it.

Messages you send

The text of a scratch card travels inside the iMessage itself, protected by Apple’s iMessage encryption. It is never sent to us or stored on any server of ours. We have no copy.

Photos behind the foil

You can hide a photo behind the silver. Here is exactly how that works:

  • The photo is encrypted on your device (AES‑256) before it goes anywhere.
  • The encrypted copy is stored temporarily in Apple’s iCloud (CloudKit) so the recipient can fetch it.
  • The decryption key travels only inside the iMessage itself — it is never stored with the photo and never reaches us. Only the person you sent the card to can ever decrypt it. We can’t. Nobody in between can.
  • The encrypted cloud copy deletes itself after 30 days.
  • Once opened, the photo is kept on the recipient’s device so the card keeps working, protected by iOS file encryption.

One honest caveat: like every messaging app, we can’t prevent a recipient from taking a screenshot. A scratch card is a gift, not a vault — send accordingly.

Reporting a photo

If someone sends you a photo you shouldn’t have received, long‑press it and choose report this photo. That opens an email to us containing the photo’s storage reference and its key — you are choosing to show us that one photo so we can review it and remove it. This is the only way any photo can ever become visible to us, and it is always the recipient’s choice. Material that violates law or our rules is removed within 24 hours; content involving minors is reported to the relevant authorities, including NCMEC.

What we store, where

  • On our servers: nothing. We don’t run any.
  • In Apple’s iCloud (our CloudKit container): encrypted photo blobs — unreadable without the key we never have — each with an expiry date. No names, no identifiers, no message text.
  • On your device: drafts, local settings, local counters, and opened photos — inside the app’s sandbox, none of it transmitted.

No third parties

No third-party SDKs, no data sharing, no selling — there is nothing to share or sell.

Your rights

Scratch that is made by Mindact Solutions AB, Sweden. Since we hold no personal data about you, there is nothing for us to hand over or lose — where GDPR applies you retain all your usual rights, and deletion is enforced by design through the 30‑day expiry above. If we ever change how any of this works, this page changes first — we will never quietly start collecting what we today can’t see.
